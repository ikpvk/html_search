Lion

The lion is a large cat of the genus Panthera native to Africa and India.

Scientific Classification

Scientific Name: Panthera leo
Family: Felidae
Order: Carnivora

Physical Characteristics

Lions have a muscular, deep-chested body with a short, rounded head, round ears, and a hairy tuft at the end of their tail. Adult males have a prominent mane around their neck and head. Lions are sexually dimorphic - males are larger than females and possess the iconic mane.

Habitat and Distribution

Lions historically ranged across Africa, Europe, and Asia. Today, they are found primarily in sub-Saharan Africa and a small population in western India. They inhabit savannas, grasslands, and open woodlands.

Diet

Lions are apex predators and hunt mainly large ungulates such as zebras, wildebeests, and buffalo. They are cooperative hunters and often work in groups to take down prey larger than themselves.

Behavior

Lions are the only cats that live in social groups called prides. A pride typically consists of related females and their cubs, along with a small group of adult males. They are primarily nocturnal and spend much of their time resting.
