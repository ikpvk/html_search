Tiger

The tiger is the largest living cat species and a iconic apex predator.

Scientific Classification

Scientific Name: Panthera tigris
Family: Felidae
Order: Carnivora

Physical Characteristics

Tigers have a muscular body with powerful forelimbs and a large head. Their most distinctive feature is the dark vertical stripes on their orange fur. No two tigers have the same stripe pattern. They have excellent night vision for hunting in low light.

Habitat and Distribution

Tigers are found in diverse habitats across Asia including tropical rainforests, mangroves, grasslands, and snowy coniferous forests. They historically ranged across much of Asia, but now live in fragmented populations in India, Russia, China, and Southeast Asia.

Diet

Tigers are carnivorous hunters that prey on large ungulates such as sambar deer, wild boar, and buffaloes. They are solitary hunters and rely on stealth and powerful ambush attacks. A single tiger may consume up to 40 kg of meat in one night.

Behavior

Unlike lions, tigers are mostly solitary animals. Each tiger maintains its own territory and comes together with others only for mating. They are excellent swimmers and often cool off in water during hot weather. Tigers are known to be more aggressive than lions.
